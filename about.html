Cherese Hamp was born October 4, 1994 on the south side of Chicago to Charlese Hamp and Joe Davenport. She grew up mostly on the south side with her two brothers and little sister. She attended George W Curtis Elementary School located on 32 E 115th St for kindergarten. Cherese then moved on the upper east side of Chicago where she attended Myra Bradwell Communications Arts & Sciences ES located on 7736 S Burnham Ave for 1st-4th grade.When she was in 4th grade her mom moved her and her siblings back to Englewood where she attended Oliver W. Holmes located on 955 W Garfield Blvd. Cherese attended there from 4th-6th grade before she transferred to Sherman School of Excellence located 1000 W 52nd St. Cherese attended John Hope College Prep from 9th-11th grade and graduated as Valedictorian from Neal F. Simeon Career Academy. She is part of Global Youth Leadership Institute, National Honor Society, Simeon Alumni, James R. Jordan Foundation Alumni, and many more organization. Her biggest inspiration is her younger siblings and teachers. Cherese Hamp is one of the Class of 2013 Gates Millennium Scholars.
